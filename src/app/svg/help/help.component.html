What does this page do?

The page allows you to view animations of n-dimensional cubes. This means that you can choose what dimension the cube will be in. By changing the state of the toggle switches, you can add animated rotation to any plane of the current dimension. If you expand the panel for any rotation plane (by clicking the arrow to the side), you can manually adjust the rotation and rotation velocity of that plane. Also, you can click the play/pause button at the top of the controls at anytime to stop the cube in it's tracks, reverse the rotation direction with the rewind button, and change the overall speed with the master velocity control.

Explain the rotation naming system.

It is a common misconception that rotations are performed about an axis. While rotations and an individual axis do coincide in the third dimension, where most of us spend the vast majority of their time, this doesn't extend to higher dimensions. Rather, rotations are performed on a plane consisting of two axes. To make labeling in the higher dimensions easier, axes are labeled by numbers rather than letters. These numbers apply to standard axes, with axis #1 corresponding with the X-axis, #2 with the Y-axis, and so on.
